Trades

Courses We Offer

Explore our intermediate-level courses designed to enhance your technical skills and knowledge.

Computer Operator and Programming Assistant (COPA)

Develop skills in computer operations, programming languages, database management, and networking. Learn to troubleshoot software and hardware issues and gain proficiency in office productivity tools.

Draughtsman (Civil)

Master the art of creating detailed technical drawings and plans for civil engineering projects. Learn to use computer-aided design (CAD) software and understand construction principles and materials.

Electrician

Get hands-on training in electrical systems, wiring, and installations. Acquire knowledge of electrical safety regulations, circuitry, troubleshooting techniques, and maintenance practices.

Fitter

Learn the skills required for assembling, installing, and repairing machinery and equipment. Gain knowledge of welding, cutting, and fabrication techniques, as well as precision measurement and quality control.

EMEC (Electronic Mechanic)

Acquire expertise in electronic systems and components. Learn to troubleshoot and repair electronic devices, circuit boards, communication systems, and industrial automation equipment.

Mechanic Diesel Engine

Gain knowledge of diesel engines, fuel systems, and engine components. Learn to diagnose, repair, and maintain diesel engines used in automobiles, trucks, and other heavy-duty equipment.

Dress Making

Explore the art of dressmaking and learn the techniques of designing, cutting, and stitching garments. Acquire knowledge of fabric selection, pattern making, and garment construction.

Surveyor

Develop skills in land surveying and mapping. Learn to use surveying instruments, collect data, and create accurate maps and plans for construction, infrastructure development, and land management projects.

Welder

Master the skill of welding and join different types of metals using various welding techniques. Learn to interpret blueprints, operate welding equipment, and ensure strong and durable welds.

Plumber

The Plumber trade equips students with the skills needed for the installation, maintenance, and repair of plumbing systems. Students learn about pipefitting, valve assembly, water supply systems, drainage systems, and plumbing fixtures.
